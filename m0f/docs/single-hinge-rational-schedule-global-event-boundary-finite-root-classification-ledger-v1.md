# Single-hinge rational-schedule global event-boundary finite-root classification ledger candidate v1

Status: complete exact feature classification of every finite vertex-face and
edge-edge root occurrence on every boundary of one global necessary-event
partition. The same record retains the existing left/right open-cell static
delta source. This is not persistent-event subdivision, contact policy,
collision-event deduplication, complete CCD, or global M0F `GO`.

## One central owned source

`analyzeSingleHingeRationalScheduleGlobalEventBoundaryFiniteRootClassificationLedgerV1`
invokes the global event-boundary cell-static delta ledger exactly once. That
upstream composition captures one bounded owned transition snapshot and retains
the event partition, root census, polynomial census, open-cell samples, and
static deltas.

The finite-root ledger never calls either transition-taking single-root entry
point again. It supplies the central owned polynomial census to the
compact vertex-face classifier and the central owned event-root census to the
compact edge-edge classifier. Per-occurrence results omit `polynomialCensus`
and `rootCensus`; those large sources occur only in the central delta record.
The compact records retain their selected source event and all auxiliary sign
evidence. Success and failure outputs are deeply frozen.

Revision IDs are still caller-provided labels. They are not compared with an
external reconstruction and are not cryptographic source bindings.

## Complete canonical join

Boundaries and their `finiteEventOccurrences` are traversed in their existing
canonical order. Every output row has both a global classification index and
its index within the boundary. The occurrence is joined back to the owned root
census by event index and ID, pair and triangle IDs, event kind, root index,
multiplicity, and boundary/root location.

Each vertex-face occurrence receives exactly six algebraic sign rows: three
projected face areas and three edge orientations. Each edge-edge occurrence
receives the branch-specific seven, twelve, or fourteen sign rows already
defined by its compact classifier. Returned event identity, root identity,
revision IDs, and every sign proof are checked against the source occurrence.
Any classifier or join failure rejects the whole ledger; no partial success is
returned.

Classification rows are occurrence evidence. Several necessary polynomials
may describe the same physical feature configuration at one time. Rows are not
deduplicated into collision events and category counts are not collision
counts. `outside` and `disjoint` are expected outcomes of necessary
coplanarity roots.

## Defensive limits and retained BigInt accounting

The adapter preflights at most 4,096 finite classification rows and 57,344
worst-case auxiliary sign rows. Its effective 4,097-boundary ceiling is derived
from the composed central static-delta source's 4,096-open-cell sampling bound.

The additional compact evidence may retain at most 16,777,216 BigInt bits.
Accounting recursively visits the complete compact classifier record and sums
the magnitude bit length at every property path. Zero has bit length one.
Shared object aliases are deliberately counted again when reached through a
different property, matching JSON-style expansion. Thus selected projection
aliases and the coefficient/root/refinement evidence repeated inside sign
records are included; the centrally retained source census is not counted
again. Accounting overflow or any limit breach fails closed atomically.

These are defensive experiment ceilings, not a measured SupportProfile or a
browser runtime claim.

## Fixed fixture evidence

The two-face quarter-turn fixture has 30 finite start-boundary occurrences and
none at the end: 12 vertex-face `outside` rows and 18 edge-edge `disjoint`
rows. It retains 228 auxiliary sign rows and 11,371 compact-evidence BigInt
bits. Thirty persistent events remain only in the central source.

The symmetric three-face fixture has 82 finite occurrences across start,
interior, and end boundaries in counts 58, 12, and 12. It contains 36
vertex-face and 46 edge-edge classifications, 548 auxiliary sign rows, and
136,710 compact-evidence BigInt bits. Its interior boundary includes three
vertex-face `edge`, three vertex-face `outside`, four edge-edge
`endpoint-contact`, and two edge-edge `proper-interior-crossing` outcomes. The
same boundary has only three changed static triangle-pair rows, demonstrating
why root occurrences must not be treated as deduplicated collision events.

The four-face 3/4/5 loopback fixture has 190 finite occurrences across six
boundaries: 84 vertex-face and 106 edge-edge rows. It retains 1,304 auxiliary
sign rows and 154,987 compact-evidence BigInt bits. Four duplicated
triangulation events at the start boundary reach exact `collinear-overlap`;
each retains the complete fourteen-row collinear proof. These four occurrence
rows are deliberately not deduplicated into one physical overlap event.

## Claim boundary

The ledger classifies every finite occurrence exposed by the current necessary
event family. Identically zero event polynomials are persistent over the whole
unit interval and have no discrete root occurrence; they remain in the source
record without feature-change subdivision. The result also does not establish
open-cell strata constancy, prove that a left/right sample difference occurs at
the boundary, determine approach versus separation, or apply declared-hinge,
legal-contact, layer-order, or penetration policy.

The necessary polynomial family is not established as collision-complete, and
the ledger does not provide continuous collision detection or a final
self-intersection decision. Collision freedom, SupportProfile, verification,
scientific claims, and global M0F `GO` therefore remain false.
